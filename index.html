AquaSentry: The Build

A detailed look at the physical components, robust mechanical design, and prototype timeline.

1. Materials & Components (Bill of Materials)

Sensing Probes

**Core Measurement:** pH probe, TDS (conductivity) sensor, Turbidity sensor, and DS18B20 temperature sensor.

Placed at the sensing port near the tap outlet for real-time flow analysis.

Controller & Display

**Processing & Interface:** Arduino Nano (or ESP32), 1.3″ OLED (128×64) for verdict, 4 tactile buttons (Prev, Next, Live, Prev-Day).

Reads data, executes verdict logic, and drives the local UI.

Autonomous Power

**Sustainability:** 18650 Li-ion cell, TP4056 charger, MT3608 boost converter (3.7 → 5V). Optional mini 5V/1-3W solar panel.

Designed for low-power operation and charging in remote areas.

Storage & Enclosure

**Protection & Logging:** microSD module for CSV logs, cheap plastic box with silicone sealing, rubber grommets for cable management.

Focus on robustness, low cost, and ease of maintenance access.

2. Mechanical Placement & Installation

Fluid Dynamics & Sensor Housing

The device must be mounted using a **T-joint or clamp** to the main water pipe, *never* by drilling the tap itself. The key is the **PVC sensor sleeve**: this short standoff pipe ensures the probe is constantly immersed and sits directly in the **flowing stream** when the water is running.

  • **Box Location:** Elevated beside the tap or handpump, with the screen facing the user for immediate verdict visibility.
  • **Solar Placement:** On the tank roof or a nearby dedicated pole, ensuring clear, unobstructed sunlight exposure.
  • **Maintenance Access:** Lid is easily removable. The probe must be **removable** for monthly calibration and cleaning.

3. Prototype Build Plan (Estimated 8–12 Weeks)

MVP Parts Buy & Bench Test

**Duration:** 1–2 weeks. **Goal:** Verify all sensors (pH, TDS, Turbidity) function accurately against known lab water samples and standards.

Prototype PCB/Perfboard Mount + UI

**Duration:** 1–2 weeks. **Goal:** Integrate Controller, OLED, Buttons, and SD logging module. Establish reliable hourly data logging logic.

Casing & Field Mock

**Duration:** 1 week. **Goal:** Build the final water-safe enclosure. Prepare the PVC sensor sleeve and cable gland seals for practical field use.

Field Pilot Deployment

**Duration:** 4–6 weeks. **Goal:** Install at 2 representative sites. Collect data, monitor power consumption, and conduct parallel sampling against certified lab tests.

Refinement & Scale Prep

**Duration:** 4–8 weeks. **Goal:** Finalize verdict thresholds, optimize power modes, create local assembly instructions, and establish the maintenance SOP.

GOAL: Ready for meaningful pilot deployment within 8–12 weeks.
